Race Format

The Milan to Rome relay race is a challenging yet exciting event that can be completed by teams of six - ten.

The idea behind this run is to divide the 630-kilometer path into stages, and each team member will run one or more of these stages as a relay race.

Teams can divide the path in any way they like, depending on their strengths. The ultimate goal is for all runners to reach the Colosseum in Rome, regardless of the time taken or how they got there.

Teams work together, strategize, and support each other throughout the entire journey, making it an excellent opportunity for team building and bonding.

Why?

The run between Milan and Rome is a charitable event that aims to raise funds for various sport associations.

The event organizers have set up a crowdfunding campaign to encourage people to partecipate collecting money to devolve to various sports associations with the aim of building sport infrastructures of free access.

Participating, donating, filing, advertising, everyone can make a difference and help support those who need it most.
